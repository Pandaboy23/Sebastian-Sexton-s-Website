Early Life


David Robert Jones, profesionaly know as David Bowie was born in Brixton, London on the 8th January, 1947 just after World War II. He grew up in Bromley, Kent. At school his dancing at school was thought to be strikingly imaginative. Later on his father brought him a collection of American 45s by artists including the Teenagers, the Platters, Fats Domino, Elvis Presley and Little Richard. Upon listening to Elvis's song "Tutti Frutti", Bowie would later say that he had "heard God". This is what most people would say inspired him. He was a very good singer and performer.



Hits and Awards


David bowie’s first hit album was Hunky Dory, which came 3rd in UK. He also won 4 brit awards and 6 grammy awards. In 2016 Bowie won the classic rock award for reissue of the Year with Scary Monsters (and Super Creeps).



Starman - Musical Analysis


The song follows a verse - chorus structure. Starman follows the 4/4 common time signature at 103 BPM. Instrumentation- Instruments include: Electric Guitar, Acoustic Guitar, Vocals, Bass Guitar, Drums and Piano. There is a instrument (Guitar) solo in the song. Texture- There is a thick texture within his songs as the instruments play repeating patterns and often play them at the same time. Harmony- The Chords include G, Cadd9, D, Dsus, Em, Bm and C.


Starman - Rock Analysis


Most of Bowie’s songs include energetic delivery of vocals. The song follows a verse - chorus structure. Starman follows the 4/4 common time. The song does not follow the normal rock tempo 140+ bpm but instead follows 103 bpm. Instruments include: Electric Guitar, Acoustic Guitar, Vocals, Bass Guitar, Drums and Piano. There is a instrument (Guitar) solo in the song. Unlike some rock songs Bowie would not use call and response. He would have strong back beat usually on bar 2 and 4 similar to many rock songs. David Bowie would not use the use of ‘stop time’ where instruments play only on beat 1 then leave space for vocals. He would use a verse-chorus form in some of his music such as in the song ‘Where are we now’ Sometimes he would implement a solo into his piece of music such as a guitar solo as enjoyed playing guitar. Unlike many rock songs David Bowie would not use a simple chord progression as he liked to make his pieces for extravagant with structures such as grace notes.
